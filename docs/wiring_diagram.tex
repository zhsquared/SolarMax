% ---- explanatory text in the (previously empty) right margin ----
\node[align=left, font=\scriptsize, anchor=north west, text width=6.2cm] at (19.6,0) {%
\textbf{How the safety chain works}\\[3pt]
\textbf{Main switch} breaks 12\,V+ to everything (ON/OFF).\\[3pt]
\textbf{E-stop} (red mushroom, NC) sits in the relay \emph{coil}: pressing it — or any
broken wire — drops the coil, the relay opens, and \textbf{motor power is cut}. Fail-safe.
The self-locking worm gear then holds the panel.\\[3pt]
The E-stop button carries only the small coil current; the \textbf{40\,A relay} carries the
$\sim$19\,A motor current. Use \textbf{12\,AWG} on the motor path.\\[6pt]
\textbf{Powering / programming}\\[3pt]
The ESP32 runs from the buck via its \textbf{5V pin}. The \textbf{Schottky diode} lets you
plug USB in anytime to flash without conflict. \textbf{USB = programming only.}};
